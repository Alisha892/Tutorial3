Technology Advancements

Since, the world is now working on a advanced technology the trend for the watches have changed from pocket watch to smartwatches.

The evolution of watch designs over the decades has been a fascinating journey, marked by technological advancements, changing fashion trends, and evolving consumer preferences. Let’s explore the key milestones in watch design from the early 20th century to the present day:



  • Early 1900s: Pocket Watches

    • During early 20s the pocket watches were at trend.They featured intricate engravings, often made of the precious metals,and had a classic round shape. Pocket watches were primarily carried in a person's pocket attached with the chain.

  • 1920s-1930s: Art Deco Influence

    • Watches were made with the influence of art deco during this period. Watches became smaller and more streamlined, with geometric shapes and bold, symmetrical patterns. Rectangular and square cases gained popularity, and dial designs became more minimalist and sophisticated.

  • 1940s-1950s: World War II and utility watches

    • During World War II, wristwatches gained prominence due to their practicality in military operations. Utility watches, also known as “military watches,” were designed for legibility and durability. They typically had larger cases, luminous dials, and robust construction.

  • 1960s-1970s: Space age and quartz revolution

    • During this period the quartz technology revolutionalized watch design were at trend. The watches were made with the aesthtetic design with assymetrical cases, bold colors, and unconventional materials.Quartz brought high accuracy with affordable timekeeping.

  • 1980s-1990s: Luxury and Sports Watches

    • This period of time witnessed the rise of luxury watches.Brands like Rolex, Omega, and Cartier were launched. Simultaneously, sports watches gained popularity, featuring enhanced water resistance, chronograph functions, and robust designs.

  • 2000s: Digital and smartwatches

    • This era was known for the digital watches and the emergence of the smartwatches. Digital watches were introduced with the features like alarms, timers, and multiple time zones. With technological advancements, smartwatches integrated features like fitness tracking, smartphone connectivity, and app compatibility.

  • 2010s-Present: Return to classic and hybrid designs

    • In recent years, there has been a resurgence of classic and vintage-inspired watch designs. Many brands introduced reissued models, paying homage to their historical timepieces. Additionally, hybrid watches gained popularity, combining traditional mechanical movements with smart functionalities.
